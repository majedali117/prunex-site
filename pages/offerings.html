Our Offerings

What Prunex Offers

Prunex helps enterprises strengthen AI governance through structured policy control, compliance support, responsible AI oversight, and continuous auditability. Each capability is designed to work independently or as part of a unified governance programme.

Offering 01

AI Policy Governance Layer

Prunex provides a centralised policy control layer that helps enterprises govern AI interactions consistently across teams, systems, and environments.

Centralised policy definitions with versioning and change tracking
Governance applied across prompts, responses, tool calls, and data flows
Strengthened oversight across enterprise AI usage, from copilots to agent-based systems
Scope-based controls by team, environment, use case, or risk level
Example policies
No customer PII in AI prompts. No medical advice without human review. No model access without role-based authorisation. No sensitive data sent to third-party AI tools.
Offering 02

Compliance and Auditability

Prunex helps organisations improve governance visibility and maintain structured, audit-ready records of AI system operations and policy enforcement.

Improved governance visibility across AI interactions and decision points
Structured audit-ready records with full decision context and policy references
Support for accountability programs and internal compliance reviews
Exportable evidence packages for regulators and external auditors
Supported frameworks
EU AI Act, GDPR, HIPAA, NIST AI RMF, ISO/IEC 42001, SOC 2, and sector-specific regulatory requirements.
Offering 03

Responsible AI Oversight

Prunex helps enable stronger ethical and governance safeguards across AI operations, supporting detection of problematic outputs and continuous oversight.

Support for detecting bias, discrimination, and problematic AI outputs
Strengthened ethical and governance safeguards embedded in standard operations
Continuous oversight of AI behaviour, decisions, and outputs
Configurable thresholds and escalation workflows for policy violations
Detection examples
Biased hiring recommendations. Sensitive medical data in outputs. Hallucinated legal citations. Discriminatory language patterns.
