# Prunex – Context Document

## Overview

Prunex is an **AI Policy Governance Gateway** designed for enterprises to bring **control, compliance, auditability, and trust** to AI systems.

It acts as a **runtime enforcement layer** between users, enterprise systems, AI models, and AI agents—ensuring that AI usage is governed not just by static policies, but by **real-time, enforceable controls**.

---

## Core Problem

Enterprises are rapidly adopting AI tools (e.g., LLMs, copilots, agentic systems), but governance has not kept pace.

Current state:
- AI policies exist as documents (PDFs, wikis, guidelines)
- Employees use AI tools in real workflows
- No real-time enforcement of policies
- Limited visibility into AI interactions
- No reliable audit trail

This creates a **governance gap**:

> The difference between having a policy and actually applying it at runtime.

### Key Risks

- Sensitive data leakage into AI tools
- Lack of traceability of AI-assisted decisions
- No enforcement of usage policies
- Compliance exposure (GDPR, EU AI Act, etc.)
- Inability to provide audit evidence
- Black-box AI usage on top of legacy systems

---

## Vision

To enable **trusted, scalable AI adoption** by transforming governance from:

**Static documentation → Runtime infrastructure**

Prunex envisions a future where:
- AI policies are **enforced automatically**
- AI interactions are **observable and auditable**
- Organizations can **prove compliance, not just declare it**
- AI systems operate within **clear, controlled boundaries**

## Solution

Prunex provides a **runtime AI governance layer** that sits between:

**Users ↔ Enterprise Systems ↔ AI Models ↔ AI Agents**

It ensures that every interaction is:
- inspected
- evaluated
- controlled
- logged

---

## How Prunex Works

Prunex follows a simple operational model:

### 1. Inspect
Capture AI interactions in real time:
- prompts
- inputs
- outputs
- context

### 2. Evaluate
Apply policies and rules:
- data sensitivity checks
- compliance rules
- responsible AI filters
- contextual risk analysis

### 3. Enforce
Take action based on policies:
- allow
- block
- redact
- warn
- modify

### 4. Audit
Create immutable logs:
- full trace of interactions
- decisions and policy outcomes
- audit-ready evidence
